Climate Prediction API

Column Descriptions

%_CHANGE_GHG - This column represents the percentage change per year in the total radiative forcing by all long-lived greenhouse gases since the pre-industrial era.

CFCS - This column represents the total radiative forcing (expressed in joules per year) exerted by chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), a type of halogenated compound that is a long-lived greenhouse gas. Increases in HCFCs and HFCs have offset the decline from CFCs so that radiative forcing from the sum of these three chemical classes has increased.

CO2 - This column represents the total radiative forcing (expressed in joules per year) exerted by carbon dioxide (CO2), a long-lived greenhouse gas. The concentration of CO2 in the atmosphere has increased mainly due to human activity since the industrial revolution.

CO2_CONCENTRATION - This column represents the annual CO2 mole fraction increase, expressed as a mole fraction in dry air (parts per million, or ppm). This measure is subject to change and recalibration based on the reference gas mixtures used and other quality control procedures.

GHG_INCREASE - This column represents the Annual Greenhouse Gas Index (AGGI), a measure of the climate-warming influence of long-lived trace gases in the atmosphere and how that influence has changed since the onset of the industrial revolution.

HYDROCHLOROFLUOROCARBONS - This column represents the total radiative forcing (expressed in joules per year) exerted by hydrochlorofluorocarbons (HCFCs), a type of halogenated compound that is a long-lived greenhouse gas. Increases in HCFCs and HFCs have offset the decline from CFCs so that radiative forcing from the sum of these three chemical classes has increased.

HYDROFLUOROCARBONS - This column represents the total radiative forcing (expressed in joules per year) exerted by hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), a type of halogenated compound that is a long-lived greenhouse gas. Increases in HCFCs and HFCs have offset the decline from CFCs so that radiative forcing from the sum of these three chemical classes has increased.

METHANE - This column represents the total radiative forcing (expressed in joules per year) exerted by methane (CH4), a long-lived greenhouse gas. The atmospheric burden of methane has increased rapidly over recent years, with causes for the increase not fully understood.

NITROUS_OXIDE - This column represents the total radiative forcing (expressed in joules per year) exerted by nitrous oxide (N2O), a long-lived greenhouse gas. The atmospheric burden of nitrous oxide continues to grow over time, with its annual increase also increasing.

SURFACE_TEMPERATURE - This column represents the global surface temperature anomaly, calculated with respect to the 1901-2000 average. It is a measure of the deviation of the earth's surface temperature from a baseline. The data come from NOAA's Global Surface Temperature Analysis, which uses comprehensive data collections of increased global coverage over land and ocean surfaces.

TOTAL_GREENHOUSE_GASES - This column represents the total radiative forcing (expressed in joules per year) by all long-lived greenhouse gases since the pre-industrial era.

TOTAL_HEAT_ABSORBED_GHG - This column represents the total heat (expressed in joules per year).
